Ownership is central to Rust's memory model. Instead of relying only on a garbage collector or manual allocation discipline, Rust checks ownership and borrowing rules during compilation.

The practical effect is that some classes of lifetime and aliasing mistakes become compile-time errors, at the cost of a steeper learning curve.
